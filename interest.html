Purpose of the Borrowing
What were the borrowed funds used for?

The tax treatment of interest depends entirely on why the money was borrowed. Select the category that best describes the use of the funds.

  • Trading purposes — e.g. bank overdraft to purchase stock, fund working capital (s.81 TCA 1997)
  • Rental property — purchase, improve or repair rented premises (s.97 TCA 1997)
  • Acquire shares / lend to companies — invest in or lend to qualifying companies (s.247 TCA 1997)
  • Qualifying financing — on-lend external borrowings to 75% subsidiaries for trade (s.76E TCA 1997)
2
Wholly & Exclusively for Trade?
s.81 TCA 1997 — Trading deduction test
📖 s.81 TCA 1997

Interest is allowed as a trading expense only if it was incurred wholly and exclusively for the purposes of the trade. See MacAonghusa v Ringmahon Co for guidance.

3
Is the Lender a Connected Company?
s.817C TCA 1997 — Unpaid interest restriction
📖 s.817C TCA 1997

Where interest is due on loans between connected companies, a deduction is only allowed to the extent the interest is actually paid (and thus taxed in the lender's hands). Unpaid interest accrued but not remitted is restricted until paid.

Under s.10 TCA 1997, a company is connected with another if one controls the other or both are under the control of the same person/company. The restriction does not apply if the lender is non-resident and not controlled by Irish residents (s.817C(2A)).
4
Was the Loan Used to Acquire Assets from a Connected Company?
s.840A TCA 1997 — Asset acquisition restriction
📖 s.840A TCA 1997

S.840A restricts interest deductions on loans from a connected company used to acquire assets from the same or another connected company. This applies across all Schedule D cases (Case I/II, III, IV, V).

The restriction does not apply to:
  • Share capital in a company
  • Intangible assets qualifying under s.291A TCA 1997
  • Assets acquired as trading stock
  • Acquisition of a trade
  • Acquisition of a leased asset
  • Loans financed by a third party (subject to anti-avoidance)
  • Interest paid to a s.110 securitisation company
  • Loans from a qualifying financing company
2
Was the Loan Used to Purchase, Improve or Repair the Premises?
s.97 TCA 1997 — Rental interest deduction
📖 s.97 TCA 1997

Interest is deductible against Case V rental income only if the borrowed money was used to purchase, improve or repair the rented premises. Interest on loans for other purposes (e.g. defending a lawsuit) is not deductible.

3
Is the Tenancy Registered with the RTB?
Residential tenancy registration requirement

For residential rental properties, the tenancy must be registered with the Residential Tenancies Board (RTB). Interest relief is denied for any property where the tenancy is not registered.

3
Anti-Avoidance: Circular Flows?
s.247(4A) TCA 1997
📖 s.247(4A) TCA 1997

Interest will not qualify as a charge where the borrower is connected with the lender and uses the funds to purchase shares of a company from a connected company. This prevents circular flows of borrowed money around a group.

The restriction does not apply if:
  • The funds are ultimately used for trading purposes (with no arrangements to repay the original loan); or
  • The borrowings create taxable income (e.g. interest income, but not dividend income).
4
Connected Loan for Asset Acquisition?
s.247(4E) TCA 1997
📖 s.247(4E) TCA 1997

Interest is restricted where: a connected company lends to the investing company, which on-lends to another company that acquires assets from a company connected with the investing company at the time of first acquisition.

The following assets are excluded from the restriction:
  • Share capital
  • Intangible assets under s.291A TCA 1997
  • Assets acquired as trading stock
Also excluded: loans to acquire a trade from a non-CT company, and leased assets not previously used by a CT company.
5
Is the Interest Treated as a Distribution?
s.130(2)(d) TCA 1997
📖 s.130 TCA 1997

Certain interest payments are deemed distributions and are therefore non-deductible. Does any of the following apply?

Interest is treated as a distribution where:
  • The loan is convertible into unquoted shares (s.130(2)(d)(ii))
  • Interest is dependent on company results (s.130(2)(d)(iii)(I))
  • Interest exceeds a commercial rate of return (s.130(2)(d)(iii)(II))
  • Interest on securities held by a non-resident parent/subsidiary (s.130(2)(d)(iv)) — but see elections below
Exemption: s.130(2B) exempts non-trade interest paid to EU/UK resident companies from s.130(2)(d)(iv).
Election: s.452 allows an election to treat trade interest under s.130(2)(d)(iv) as non-distribution (deductible).
